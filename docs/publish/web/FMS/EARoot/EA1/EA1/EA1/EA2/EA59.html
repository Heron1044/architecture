<b>Financial Management Services (FMS) Architecture Model</b><br/><br/><b>Overview</b><br/>This model consists of three application layer architecture diagrams which depict the evolution of the FMS over a 5 year time period. The FMS is intended to realized a technical solution to the MITA Financial Management business process area (or domain). <br/><br/><ul>
<li>Year 1<br/>In this time period the primary goal is to build the Federal Draw and Reporting Web application for the Accounts Receivable Management domain as represented in "The DHCS ENterprise" Business Process Area owners document. During the design and implementation of this project a domain driven design approach will be taken to identify modules for reuse in other enterprise systems. DHCS has also adopted an API First approach to system design and integration which will result in the modularity in configuration and deployment of services that are forecast to be built in years 3 & 5.<br/></li><li>Year 3<br/>This year builds upon the work in Year 1 and expands to integrate other FMS applications into the architecture.  This expands the number of applications to 3, APIs to 3 and foundational services to 7.<br/></li><li>Year 5<br/>By this year the FMS architecture will fully expand to support all of the business domain applications that are known (at this time). The full set of foundational services is made available for API composition and client integration based on the business domain.</li></ul>
<br/>The resulting architecture consists of applications, composite APIs and foundational services. Each API will be supported by a <i>service implementation</i> or a <i>product integration</i> which in turn exposed as an API. These APIs are composed into a single client facing API based on the business domain. <br/><br/>Over the 5 year life span of the FMS domain the most accurate architecture model will be the 1 year view with enough detail in the 3-5 year view to inform near term technical decisions based on anticipated business domain need. As the work in the FMS domain progresses the architecture will be refactored to reflect actual implementation and modified to reflect new facts in the business domain. <br/><br/>
